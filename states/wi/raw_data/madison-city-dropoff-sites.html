Ballot Drop-Off Sites

Voters wishing to return their absentee ballots in person may do so at one of our ballot drop-off sites.  Locations, dates, and hours will be added to this website as they are confirmed by the City Clerk's Office, so check for an updated list when ballots are mailed September 17. 

Ballot Drop-Off Sites with a Witness Available

A poll worker wearing a high visibility vest will be present outside the following locations to accept your absentee ballot and to serve as your witness, if needed, at the following locations (weather permitting).  Voters who need a witness should bring their blank ballot and absentee certificate envelope from home.

City Clerk's Office

210 Martin Luther King Jr Blvd
Weekdays, September 22 - November 2, 8 a.m. - 4:30 p.m.
 

Library Dream Bus

Look for the poll worker alongside the Library Dream Bus at these locations, only at the times listed:

  • 7933 Tree Lane, Mondays in Sept & Oct, 12-12:45 p.m.
  • The Crossings, 1128 Morraine View Dr, Mondays in Sept & Oct,1-2 p.m.
  • ReNew, 6723 Schroeder Rd, Mondays in Sept & Oct, 2:15-3:15 p.m.
  • Leopold Elementary School, 2602 Post Rd, Tuesdays in Sept & Oct, 11 a.m. - noon
  • Bridge - Lakepoint - Waunona Community Center, 1917 Lake Point Dr, Tuesdays in Sept & Oct, 12:15-1:15 p.m.
  • Mendota Elementary School, 4002 School Rd, Wednesdays in Sept & Oct, 1-2 p.m.
  • Kennedy Heights Community Center, 199 Kennedy Heights, Wednesdays in Sept & Oct, 2:15-3:15 p.m.
  • Oak Park Terrace, 571 Branding Ln, Wednesdays in Sept & Oct, 3:30-4 p.m.
  • Sandburg Elementary School, 4114 Donald Dr, Thursdays in Sept & Oct, 11:30 a.m. - noon
  • East Madison Community Center, 8 Straubel Ct, Thursdays in Sept & Oct, 2-2:30 p.m.
  • Allied Drive Learning Center, 2237 Allied Dr, Fridays in  Sept & Oct, 1-1:45 p.m.
  • Owl Creek, 23 Horned Owl Dr, Fridays in Sept & Oct, 2:15-3 p.m.

Democracy in the Park

Poll workers wearing high visibility vests will be present to accept your absentee ballot and to serve as your witness, if needed, at all City of Madison community, neighborhood, and mini parks from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. on Saturday, September 26, and from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. on Saturday, October 3.  Just look for the Vote yard sign at the park.  If we have inclement weather, the rain date will be the following day.  BYOB (bring your own ballot).  Voters who need a witness should bring their blank ballot and absentee certificate envelope from home.
